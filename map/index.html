Datasets

This project compiled and analyzed datasets including quantitative and qualitative information on the transnational circulation and control of small arms, with a specific focus on military-style rifles in Latin America. They include data from official national and international sources—such as customs records, national reports, and court documentation—as well as social media user-generated postings that capture relevant contextual, sociocultural, economic, and political dynamics. This collection not only maps both the legal trade and illicit trafficking routes, but also sheds light on patterns of arms diversion and cultural expressions related to small arms use, providing a foundation for analyzing trends in violence and informing policy recommendations.

United Nations Commodity Trade Statistics Database (UN Comtrade)

The United Nations Commodity Trade Statistics Database (UN Comtrade) is a repository of international trade data reported by national authorities. It includes detailed information on the volumes and values of goods exchanged between countries, offering insights into both the legal export and import of weapons, broken down into broad subcategories. In this study, UN Comtrade data helps mapping the legal transnational flows of small arms both within Latin America and between the region and the rest of the world. By analyzing trade records from UN Comtrade, we identified patterns, tracked the movement of arms across borders, and assessed how official trade channels contribute to or diverge from broader patterns of arms circulation, thereby complementing other data sources on illicit flows and regulatory controls.

United Nations Register of Conventional Arms (UNROCA)

The United Nations Register of Conventional Arms (UNROCA) is a global transparency mechanism tracking international transfers of major conventional weapons and small arms. While its coverage is limited as it relies on voluntary reporting by states, this dataset offers valuable insights into legal arms transfers worldwide, especially when reports include details about the makes and models transferred. In this study, UNROCA data was utilized to triangulate other sources on the authorized trade and to further document the makes and models of firearms transferred legally in Latin America.

Arms Trade Treaty (ATT)

The Arms Trade Treaty (ATT) is an international instrument adopted by the UN General Assembly to regulate the transfer of conventional arms, including small arms and light weapons. Although the ATT has not been signed and ratified by all countries, its annual reports provide a useful indicator on conventional arms exports, imports, and transfers, allowing researchers to track the legal circulation of small arms across borders.

UNODC Global Study on Firearms Trafficking

The Global Study on Firearms Trafficking is a global data-collection effort lead by the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC). It includes data on firearm seizures, trafficking incidents, and related enforcement reported by over 100 countries and territories.

National statistics

National statistics from institutions such as Brazil’s Federal Police Ministry and various state-level law enforcement agencies, Mexico’s Secretariat of National Defense (SEDENA), and Colombia’s National Administrative Department of Statistics (DANE) provide detailed insights into the local dimensions of arms circulation and control. For this research, these datasets were compiled following freedom of information requests (FOIA) to offer detailed records of legal arms transfers, seizures, and security incidents. These statistics serve as a valuable complement to international data by shedding light on country-specific trends, regulatory practices, and the challenges inherent in tracking small arms flows.

ImportGenius

ImportGenius is a commercial trade intelligence platform that compiles detailed shipment records, including container manifests and customs data, from a range of global sources. We leveraged Large Language Models (LLMs) to consolidate and standardize data for selected countries to ensure higher consistency. In this study, ImportGenius data were used to triangulate other sources on the authorized trade and garner more detailed information on the transferred weapons, when available.

Social media images

Images posted on social media can reflect the users’ thoughts, opinions, and experiences with military-style rifles. We analyzed images featuring military-style rifles on Instagram and Telegram. The online representations often convey specific gendered political meanings, either reinforcing and contesting ideals of masculinity. This is further intertwined with a military aesthetic that not only mirrors the functional use of assault rifles by state, cartel, or paramilitary groups but is also articulated through distinct styles of clothing and body postures. Complementing these is a sensuous dimension—often termed “gun porn”—which arranges the weaponry in ways that evoke both excitement and power, a visual strategy frequently encountered in police seizures.
