We are an international consortium analyzing the circulation of military-style assault rifles in Latin America, the adoption and implementation of national and multilateral control norms and their effects in the region’s gun culture

POLICY AND RESEARCH INTERFACE

We propose to analyze the circulation of military-style small arms in Latin America and the adoption and implementation of national and multilateral control norms. Through our mixed-methods research, we aim to identify the main challenges and opportunities for international cooperation for the prevention of flows and uses of rifles that hinder human centered development.

CONSORTIUM

Funded by the Swiss Network of International Studies (SNIS), we are a diverse group of practitioners and scholars with expertise in criminology, law, international relations, sociology and anthropology. Our principal members are part of the Small Arms Survey, the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro’s Digital Humanities Lab, and the Geneva Graduate Institute.
